About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Physics at the University of Houston, working under the supervision of Dr. Shuo Chen. My research takes an interdisciplinary approach, blending advanced microscopy techniques to understand and optimize novel functional materials for superconductivity and thermoelectric applications.

Currently, I am in the final phase of my Ph.D. and am actively seeking roles in the energy, thermal, climate, and AI domains.

My research interests include:

  • Thermoelectric Materials
  • Superconductors
  • Advanced Microscopy Techniques
  • Thermal Science & Energy Systems
  • Optimization & AI for Materials

If you'd like to collaborate, feel free to get in touch.

Background

I completed my Master’s degree in Physics before pursuing doctoral research at the University of Houston. My experimental track focuses on processing, structural characterization, and property measurements of high-efficiency materials.
