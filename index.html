About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Physics at the University of Houston working under the supervision of Dr. Shuo Chen. My research takes an interdisciplinary approach, blending advanced microscopy techniques to understand and optimize novel functional materials.

My research interests include:

  • High-Temperature Superconductivity
  • Thermoelectric Materials
  • Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM)
  • Functional Oxide Thin Films
  • Defect Engineering
  • Thermal Managment

If you'd like to collaborate, feel free to get in touch. I'm always excited about studing the functional materials at microscopic level with advanced microscopy techniques.

Background

I grew up in Pakistan and completed my prior degrees in physics before pursuing graduate study at the University of Houston.My academic training spans condensed matter physics, materials science, and advanced microscopy techniques for material study.
